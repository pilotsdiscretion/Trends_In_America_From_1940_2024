Sources & Citations

Authoritative references backing every data series in “Trends in America From 1940–2024.”

Compiled: June 15, 2026 Companion file: Accuracy_Review_and_Corrections.html Citations: 80+ primary sources across 10 thematic areas

Purpose. This dossier documents the primary source behind each data series in the infographic, so that any figure can be traced to the responsible statistical authority. Every entry lists the organization (and why it is authoritative), the exact dataset or report, the author(s) and their qualifications, the publication date, a working link, and which numbers it supports.

Where the document's figure diverges from the source, that is noted in the companion Accuracy Review & Corrections file rather than here. Source type is color-tagged: FEDERAL AGENCY official U.S. government statistics PEER-REVIEWED academic journal RESEARCH ORG nonpartisan institute / NGO INDUSTRY commercial / corporate data

§Primary authorities at a glance

Most series rest on a U.S. federal statistical agency. These agencies are the legally designated, methodologically peer-reviewed sources of record for their domains; their data is in the public domain and is the standard reference in litigation, congressional testimony, and scholarship.

On authorship and qualifications. For U.S. federal statistical products, authorship is institutional: the data is produced by career statisticians and epidemiologists within the agency under standardized, externally reviewed methodologies, and is the official record for its subject. Where a specific named author or analyst is credited on a report (e.g., NCHS data-brief authors, Pew and Gallup analysts, or the authors of a peer-reviewed study), that name and their stated credentials are listed in the entry. Peer-reviewed journal articles (JAMA, NEJM, AJCN, AJPH, CA: A Cancer Journal for Clinicians) carry the additional qualification of independent scientific peer review.

Link integrity. All URLs were retrieved during verification in June 2026. Government statistical pages are periodically reorganized; if a deep link has moved, the figure can be relocated via the agency's main data portal (e.g., cdc.gov/nchs, census.gov, bls.gov, seer.cancer.gov). No URL in this dossier was generated without retrieval.
